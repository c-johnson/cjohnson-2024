In June of 2012, the World Energy Project will be sending a team back to Mali to install a solar power system on the Koutiala Hospital for Women and Children. In 2011 when we were on the ground in Mali, we had the opportunity to work with a Dutch engineer and the hospital staff to develop a solution to the hospital’s energy concerns.


Currently, the power company in Koutiala schedules outages on a near daily basis. During these times, the hospital must run a back-up diesel generator which is expensive but necessary considering the hospital’s importance in impacting the health of local residents.


With a reliable solar power system, the hospital can save up to $2000 a month on their electric bill! The money that they save can be better spent purchasing new medical equipment, assisting in patient medical bills, or hiring new staff. The World Energy Project will provide assistance in fundraising for the solar installation, and in June install the system. We are very excited about the opportunity to revisit Koutiala next summer!












The World Energy Project is working with the Friends of St. Anne's Girl's School in Kapkemich, Kenya to improve the electricity situation at the elementary school. Friends of St. Anne's Girl's School is a non-profit based in Kansas City that has supported the school since 2007, and we are honored to work with them on developing an energy system for the school.

In July 2011, we sent Eric Svingen to Kapkemich to visit with the community and to analyze the school so that we can better address its energy needs. During the summer of 2012, Eric will lead a team back to Kapkemich to install a solar photovaltaic system to power the school.

To get updates on this project, follow our blog!


In June of 2011, our team traveled to Mali, West Africa to complete The World Energy Project’s flagship project. During the three weeks we were there, we learned a lot about photovoltaics, irrigation, and the Malian culture.


While there our team:


  • Installed solar panels and a lightning protection system on a school
  • Manufactured frames for a photvoltaic system for a children’s home
  • Installed a drip-irrigation system for a community garden and sowed the field with corn
  • Met with the Dean of the College of Science and Technology at the University in Bamako to discuss scholarship opportunities
  • Visited a 72kw Solar Array at Kimparana that powers 325+ homes
  • Visited a community farm outside of Mopti to research design methods for community gardens
