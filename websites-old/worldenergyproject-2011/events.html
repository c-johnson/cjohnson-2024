On May 3rd, our good friends at Global Partners in Hope will premiere a documentary showcasing the celebration and dedication of their latest project in the Bako region of Mali, West Africa.

In February, GPiH, along with representatives from Omaha Public Schools and Journey Church, opened a new medical clinic and well that will serve more than 14,000 people in the region. Along with clean water and basic medical care, the project launched the area’s first labor and delivery building.

Over the last 8 months, members of the World Energy Project have given presentations about water conservation and clean energy at these school to kick off the fundraising efforts. Come show your support while learning about the challenges of global development.

On January 26, 2011, Nate Watson of Continuum Financial organized the first WEP fundraising event at Bold Office Solutions in Omaha, Nebraska. Business professionals from the greater Omaha area met with students from UNL, UNO, and Creighton to discuss what engineering can offer the developing world and how we can use our skills to help people abroad.


The World Energy Project raised over $1,400 through generous donations from patrons, Bold Office Solutions, and Continuum. Students networked with professionals working in the fields of architecture, engineering, and information technology. Everybody had a lot of fun and everyone was excited to hear about WEP's mission.





The World Energy Project was invited to present our organization at the Water for Food Institute located in Lincoln, Nebraska. We presented our work alongside the research of University of Nebraska PHD students who are offering their own solutions to world problems. We shared our vision with the Institues directors and got a chance to engage Nebraska’s leading visionaries.
